Produce, Consult, import & Export Turkey Genetics

The domestic turkey is derived from the native wild turkey of North America. Each year 267 million­ turkeys are sold in the United States. They're bred for big breasts and their pure white feat­her. But turkeys weren't always like this and their genes have been manipulated to reach this point. The genetic potential of turkey breeds is constantly improving due to the efforts by the breeding companies and is seen across a wide range of biological traits. Exploitation of genetic potential requires constant evolution of nutrition and management systems to ensure previously accepted methods have not reached a limiting threshold. The genetic improvement in turkey is attributed largely to selective breeding programs that rely on highly heritable phenotypic traits, such as body size and breast muscle development. Also, considerable attention has been done on artificial insemination in recent years to improve their reproductive efficiency and meat production.

Produce, Consult, import & Export Sheep Genetics

Artificial insemination is the process of collecting sperm cells from a male animal and manually depositing them into the reproductive tract of a female. Artificial insemination is commonly used instead of natural mating in many species of animals because of the many benefits it can reap. These benefits include increased safety of the animals and producer, increased production efficiency and better genetics. Artificial insemination centres were founded by the state between early 1950s and the end of the 1970s. The state of artificial insemination in the sheep and goat industries has developed over the last couple of decades. The number of artificial inseminations in the sheep industry and the ratio of inseminated ewes increased sharply. As the consequences of the use of artificial insemination (AI) with semen from imported breeding rams, wool production traits (fibre diameter, shearing, greasy wool weight and staple length among others) have steadily and gradually increased. Also, average milk production per ewe has increased 6 times annually.
